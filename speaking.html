Part 1日常基础表达
Hometown
What do you like most about your hometown?
答题思路
先说最喜欢的点，再补一个具体细节，例如节奏、食物、熟悉感或交通便利。
示范回答
What I like most about my hometown is the sense of familiarity it gives me. I know the streets, the local food, and even the general rhythm of daily life there, so it always makes me feel relaxed. It’s not a huge city, but it’s convenient enough for everyday living, which is something I really appreciate.
可背表达
  • the sense of familiarity it gives me
  • the general rhythm of daily life
  • convenient enough for everyday living
Part 1日常进阶表达
Home
Do you enjoy spending time at home?
答题思路
给出明确态度，解释家里能提供什么，比如安静、效率、恢复精力。
示范回答
Yes, I do, especially when I need to recharge after a busy day. Home is the one place where I can fully relax without feeling distracted. I can read, watch something light, or just have some quiet time, so it plays an important role in helping me reset mentally.
可背表达
  • recharge after a busy day
  • fully relax without feeling distracted
  • reset mentally

Part 2人物基础表达
Describe a person who gives you useful advice
You should say who this person is, what kind of advice this person gives, when you usually talk to him or her, and explain why the advice is useful.
高分思路
选一个能长期给你建议的人。重点不是“厉害”，而是“建议具体、可执行、对你真的有帮助”。
参考答案
I’d like to talk about one of my close friends, who often gives me very practical advice. She’s the kind of person who stays calm and thinks clearly, especially when I’m feeling stressed or uncertain about something. I usually talk to her when I’m dealing with study-related pressure or when I’m struggling to make a decision. What makes her advice useful is that she never gives vague encouragement. Instead, she breaks problems down into smaller steps and helps me focus on what I can actually do next. For example, if I’m overwhelmed by deadlines, she’ll suggest a simple plan rather than telling me not to worry. I find that really helpful because it makes problems feel more manageable. I also trust her because she listens carefully before saying anything. Overall, her advice is useful not just because it sounds sensible, but because it’s realistic and easy to act on.
可背表达
  • very practical advice
  • break problems down into smaller steps
  • realistic and easy to act on
Part 2物品基础表达
Describe a useful object you use every day
You should say what it is, when you got it, how often you use it, and explain why it is useful.
高分思路
不要只说“手机”。可以强调它如何节省时间、提高效率、帮助管理生活。
参考答案
A useful object that I use every day is my laptop. I got my current one a few years ago when I needed a more reliable device for study. Since then, I’ve used it almost every single day. What makes it so useful is that it supports nearly everything I need to do, from reading articles and writing assignments to organising files and attending online meetings. It has basically become the centre of both my academic life and part of my personal life as well. I particularly value the fact that it helps me work more efficiently. Instead of switching between different devices, I can keep everything in one place and manage my tasks more smoothly. It also saves time, which matters a lot when I’m busy. Although it’s just a tool, it has become something I rely on heavily in my daily routine.
可背表达
  • supports nearly everything I need to do
  • the centre of my academic life
  • manage my tasks more smoothly
Part 2地点进阶表达
Describe a place you would like to visit again
You should say where it is, when you went there, what you did there, and explain why you want to visit it again.
高分思路
重点放在“第一次没来得及深入体验”和“第二次去会更有意义”。
参考答案
A place I’d really like to visit again is Kyoto in Japan. I went there during a relatively short trip, and although I only stayed for a few days, the city left a strong impression on me. What I liked most was its atmosphere. It felt calm, elegant, and full of small details that you could easily miss if you were in a hurry. During the trip, I visited a few temples, walked through quiet streets, and tried some local food, but I still felt I hadn’t seen enough. The pace of the journey was a bit rushed, so I didn’t have time to explore certain areas properly. That’s one of the main reasons I’d like to go back. I think it’s the kind of place that becomes more enjoyable when you travel slowly and pay closer attention to the surroundings. I’d also love to see it in a different season, because I imagine the atmosphere would feel quite different.
可背表达
  • left a strong impression on me
  • full of small details
  • travel slowly and pay closer attention
Part 2经历进阶表达
Describe a time when you were very busy
You should say when it was, what you had to do, why you were so busy, and explain how you felt about it.
高分思路
时间线要清楚：何时忙、忙什么、压力来自哪里、最后怎么想。
参考答案
One of the busiest times I can remember was during the final weeks of a university semester. At that time, I had several assignments due almost at the same time, and I was also preparing for exams. On top of that, I still had to deal with normal daily responsibilities, so it felt like everything was happening at once. Most of my day was spent reading, writing, checking data, and revising lecture materials. What made that period especially stressful was not just the workload itself, but the constant pressure of deadlines. I had to plan nearly every hour of the day just to keep up. Even though I was tired, I also felt surprisingly productive, because I could see myself making progress little by little. Looking back, it was definitely exhausting, but it taught me a lot about time management and self-discipline. It also showed me that I can handle more pressure than I sometimes expect.
可背表达
  • everything was happening at once
  • the constant pressure of deadlines
  • handle more pressure than I sometimes expect
Part 2经历基础表达
Describe a time when someone helped you
You should say when it happened, who helped you, how this person helped you, and explain how you felt about it.
高分思路
讲一个具体小场景更真实，重点写“当时很需要帮助”。
参考答案
I’d like to describe a time when one of my classmates helped me during a very stressful period at university. It happened when I was trying to finish an assignment, and I realised I had misunderstood part of the instructions. I was already short on time, so I started to panic a little. My classmate noticed that I seemed anxious and offered to go through the task with me. She explained the requirements clearly and helped me identify what needed to be changed. More importantly, she made me feel calmer, because I no longer felt I had to deal with everything on my own. I was really grateful, not only because she saved me time, but also because her support gave me confidence. It reminded me how important it is to have people around you who are willing to help when things become difficult.
可背表达
  • misunderstood part of the instructions
  • I started to panic a little
  • I no longer felt I had to deal with everything on my own

Part 3人物进阶表达
Friendship
Why is it easier for some people to make friends than others?
答题框架
观点：与性格、社交经验和环境有关。原因：外向的人更容易主动开始对话；固定环境也会帮助关系发展。
参考回答
I think it mainly depends on personality and social experience. Some people are naturally more outgoing, so they find it easier to start conversations and make others feel comfortable. By contrast, people who are more reserved may need more time before they open up. Another important factor is environment. If someone studies or works in a place where they meet the same people regularly, friendships tend to develop more naturally. So in many cases, it isn’t only about personality. Opportunity and repeated contact matter as well.
可背结构
  • I think it mainly depends on...
  • By contrast, ...
  • Opportunity and repeated contact matter as well.
Part 3科技进阶表达
Technology and Communication
Has technology improved the way people communicate?
答题框架
先肯定效率，再补充深度下降这一面，形成平衡答案。
参考回答
Overall, yes, I think technology has improved communication in terms of speed and convenience. People can now stay in touch instantly no matter where they are, which is especially helpful for long-distance relationships or international work. However, I don’t think every aspect of communication has improved. In some cases, online interaction can feel less personal, and people may rely too much on short messages rather than meaningful conversations. So I’d say technology has made communication easier, but not necessarily deeper.
可背结构
  • in terms of speed and convenience
  • However, I don’t think every aspect has improved.
  • easier, but not necessarily deeper

Part 3学习进阶表达
Education
Do you think schools should focus more on practical skills?
答题框架
最稳的是平衡答案： practical skills 很重要，但不能完全替代学科知识。
参考回答
Yes, to some extent. I think schools should place more emphasis on practical skills such as communication, financial literacy, and problem-solving, because these are directly useful in adult life. That said, I don’t think practical skills should replace academic subjects. A solid academic foundation is still important, especially for developing analytical thinking. Ideally, education should do both: provide knowledge and prepare students for real-world situations.
可背结构
  • place more emphasis on...
  • That said, ...
  • Ideally, education should do both.

Why do some people enjoy working in a team while others prefer working alone?

Part 3媒体进阶表达
Books and Media
Do you think people read less nowadays than in the past?
答题框架
可以说“传统阅读可能少了，但信息阅读并没有减少”，这样更灵活。
参考回答
In a traditional sense, I think many people probably do read fewer books than before, mainly because they now spend more time on short-form digital content. However, that doesn’t necessarily mean people are reading less overall. They still read messages, articles, captions, and online information every day. So I’d say the habit of reading hasn’t disappeared, but the format and depth of reading may have changed quite a lot.
可背结构
  • In a traditional sense, ...
  • that doesn’t necessarily mean...
  • the format and depth may have changed
Part 3社会进阶表达
Cities and Quality of Life
Why do many people choose to live in big cities even though city life can be stressful?
答题框架
答“机会”最稳：工作、教育、交通、服务资源；再补充压力来源。
参考回答
I think the main reason is opportunity. Big cities usually offer better access to jobs, education, transport, healthcare, and a wider range of services. For many people, these advantages outweigh the downsides, even if city life is fast-paced and sometimes overwhelming. In other words, although living in a large city can be stressful, people often feel that it gives them more possibilities for personal and professional development.
可背结构
  • these advantages outweigh the downsides
  • fast-paced and sometimes overwhelming
  • personal and professional development
Part 3社会基础表达
Advice
Why do young people sometimes prefer getting advice from friends rather than family?
答题框架
从“更容易共鸣”和“担心被长辈评价”两个角度讲。
参考回答
I think one reason is that friends are often at a similar stage of life, so their advice may feel more relatable. Young people may also feel less pressure when talking to friends, because they’re not worried about being judged in the same way. That doesn’t mean family advice is less valuable, but in some situations, friends simply feel easier to talk to.
可背结构
  • at a similar stage of life
  • feel more relatable
  • easier to talk to
